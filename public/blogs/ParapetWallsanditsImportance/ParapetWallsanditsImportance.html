Parapet Walls - An Intro

A parapet wall acts as a barrier which is usually an upward extension of a wall at the edge of the roof, terrace, balcony, walkway, or any other structure. The word parapet is derived from the Italian word “Parapetto” which means to cover or defend. Parapets were initially used to defend structures and buildings from enemies and military attack. But in the present world they are primarily used as guard rails, to conceal rooftop equipment, and to reduce wind loads on the roof.  Parapet walls are low structural walls built on the edge of the slab on one side to prevent people from falling off the edge of the slab or the roof.

Types of Parapet Walls:

Flat or Plain Parapet Walls:

Known to be one of the oldest yet simple types of parapet walls which is normally 3 feet in height. These walls have a vertical extension or brickwork over the edge of the slab of the balcony or terrace. These parapet walls do not have any unique aesthetic look, but one can always experiment with the material used, texture, and paint colour combinations done to them.

Perforated Parapet Walls:

Perforated parapet wall is also a vertical extension over the slab, where instead of leaving the parapet plain and solid with concrete, perforations are done between the parapet to enhance the aesthetic appearance or even for people or kids to sit through this parapet outside. Based on the theme of the building, perforations are carried out in different patterns  and designs.

Embattled Parapet Walls:

Also called the “Castle parapet wall” Embattled parapet walls were designed for castles in the ancient times. These kinds of parapet walls were mostly found in forts, monuments, and historical structures. Known for their intricate and carved work on both lower and higher portions they look really beautiful. Embattled parapet walls when built with bricks and stones can bring a great aesthetic look to the home.

Panelled Parapet Walls:

Panelled parapet walls are simple which are 3 feet in height from the slab. These parapet walls are panelled with exterior panels to give a touch to the home. They were built with gypsum or concrete in ancient times. But today, they can be done using many panels or materials like stone, tiles, or even HPL boards. They are built to uplift the overall appearance of the home. Panelled parapet walls also protect the parapet from water, heat, and dust. But one main drawback is that these panels need to be maintained from time to time based on the materials used.

Shapes of Parapet Walls for Sloped Roofs:

There is no requirement for parapet walls on the sloped roofs as most of the services are done underground. But, still to protect the parapet walls from external factors like water, sun, and wind few different shapes of parapets are used on sloped roofs which are listed below.

  • Sloped Parapet Walls

As the name suggests the parapet is built on the sloping roofs. This is almost similar to a plain or flat parapet wall on flat roofs. Sloped parapet walls are not usually used for homes, but are largely used in SSI and MSI industries and units. Sloped parapet walls also increase the height of the homes to a great extent.

  • Stepped Parapet Walls

This type of parapet walls resemble a stepped or a staircase format. The height of the parapet wall can vary depending on the design. These stepped parapet walls are not just appealing, but also provide an added safety to the home. This type of facade can be made if you are looking to add an extra touch to the home.

  • Curved Parapet Walls

This type of parapet walls have a curved design and are made with bricks or concrete. Curved parapet walls are also known as arched parapets since they look like an arch. These kinds of parapet walls are built to enhance the aesthetic look of the home.

Modern Types of Parapets Walls:

Bricks and concrete are being replaced by modern materials to create a lasting impression. Some of the modern types of parapet walls used are listed below.

  1. They give the homes a modern and sleek look without looking bulky or traditional.
  2. Users can connect with indoor and outdoor experience.
  3. Children and toddlers can enjoy an amazing outside view which they cannot do with other types of parapet walls due to their increased height.
  4. Elders and adults can also enjoy the view with their family members.
  5. The material used to build the parapet wall is the toughened glass which is tough by nature. Stainless steel is also durable and long lasting.  The toughened glass prevents any mishaps and renders good safety to the home.

  1. Metal Parapet Wall

Parapet walls built with materials made of metals are the next most preferred options. Some users opt for metal parapet walls  since the price of the toughened glass is expensive and provides less safety. Metal parapet walls has many merits like;

  1. These types of parapets offer a good connection between indoor and outdoor environments.
  2. As the parapets are made using metals, many variations in terms of perforations and patterns can be done.
  3. Elders and children can have an outside view.
  4. Metal parapet walls are less appealing  and do not provide a modern look to the home.

Following are the metals that can be used in parapet walls.

  2. Aluminium - When it comes to patterns aluminium has limited style options. The metal is expensive and is not scratch resistant.
  3. Stainless Steel - Steel is the most preferred option as it does not rust. A lot of patterns are available in terms of design and are scratch-resistant. They are lightweight, durable, and cost-effective as well.

Importance of Parapet Walls:

Some of the prime importance of parapet walls are listed below.

Parapet Walls Standard Height And Thickness

One of the important factors that one needs to consider while constructing the parapet wall is its height. The height of the parapet wall can vary depending on the building codes and regulations of the specific state or city where the house is located. However, as per the National Building Code of India (NBC), the minimum height of the parapet wall should be 1.05 metres (approx 3.44 feet) above the roof level. Again, it's important to note that the height requirements may vary depending on the specific location and building codes applicable for your project. It is always better to consult an architect or an engineer who can help you determine the appropriate height for your parapet wall based on your specific project requirements and local regulations.

The standard thickness of the parapet wall will depend on several factors such as the height of the building, the type of construction, and the loads that the wall is expected to resist. However, as per the National Building Code of India (NBC), the minimum thickness of the parapet wall should be 23 cm (9 inches) for a masonry wall and 15 cm (6 inches) for a reinforced concrete (RC) wall. The NBC also specifies that the thickness of the parapet wall should be increased in proportion to the height of the building and the loads it is expected to resist. For example, for buildings up to 10 metres in height, the thickness of the parapet wall should be increased by 10% for every additional metre of height. For buildings taller than 10 metres, the thickness should be increased by 5% for every additional metre of height.

It’s important to note that these are minimum requirements and the actual thickness of the parapet wall may need to be increased depending on the specific requirements of your project. Hence, it’s always best to consult an architect or an engineer who can help you determine the appropriate thickness for your parapet wall based on your specific project requirements and local regulations.

Why is there a need for wall coping to the parapet wall?

In order to prevent the entry of moisture it is very essential to provide wall coping to the parapet wall. It is basically a covering at the top of the wall to prevent seepage of the water. Coping to the parapet wall acts as a protection against the aggressive external environmental conditions like rain, strong winds, snow, etc. Coping also helps parapet walls to prevent rain water from penetrating into it.

Things To Be Considered While Construction of Parapet Wall

There are some factors which should be considered while construction of the parapet walls that are listed below.

  • The minimum height of the parapet wall should be approx 3.5 feet.
  • Parapet walls should have a minimum thickness of about 9 inches for masonry walls and 6 inches for reinforced Concrete (RC) walls.
  • In order to prevent the dampness, parapet walls should be plastered from both sides.
  • The joint to the roof and the parapet should be sealed in a proper manner.
  • Parapet walls need proper coping to prevent leakages.
  • No plumbing works or electrical wiring should be carried out near to the parapet walls.
