  1. Buckingham Palace| London, UK

Did you know the Buckingham Palace located in London, UK is the most expensive building in the world? Though the Queen of England is no more, she is still the queen of real estate. Yes, you read it right and it’s a fact. The world’s most iconic landmark the Buckingham Palace located in the heart of the city of London in the United Kingdom has 775 rooms, 188 staff rooms that include 52 royal and guest rooms. The palace has 92 offices, 78 bathrooms, and 19 staterooms making this not just the world’s most expensive residence but also making it the most roomy residences in the world. Although the Queen owns many castles and mansions throughout the UK, Buckingham Palace has been the official home of the monarchy since 1837.

Buckingham Palace is known to be the administrative headquarters of the monarch of the United Kingdom. Situated in the city of Westminster, Buckingham Palace is often at the center of attraction for the people of England at times of national rejoicing and during mourning’s. Originally, known as ‘Buckingham House’ the palace was a large town house built for the Duke of Buckingham in the year 1703. During the 19th century, the palace was renovated by architects who constructed three wings around a central courtyard. The front palace measures 355 feet across, by 390 feet deep, by 80 feet high and covers an area spanning across 8,30,000 sq ft. Buckingham Palace also has a post office, cinema theater, swimming pool, a medical care center, and a jewellery workshop.  The property is valued at $6.7 billion which makes it the most expensive home in the world.
